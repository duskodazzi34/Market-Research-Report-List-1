<p><ul><li>Convection Reflow Soldering</li><li>Condensation Reflow Soldering</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The reflow soldering system market encompasses various technologies for joining electronic components to circuit boards. Convection reflow soldering uses hot air or nitrogen to evenly heat the solder paste and components, ensuring consistent melting and adhesion. In contrast, condensation reflow soldering employs vapor condensation to transfer heat, providing precise temperature control and minimizing thermal stress on components. Both methods cater to different manufacturing needs, optimizing soldering quality and efficiency in various electronic assembly applications.</p></p>
