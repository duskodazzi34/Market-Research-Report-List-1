<p><p>Crystalline silicon solar modules are categorized into two primary types: monocrystalline and polycrystalline. Monocrystalline solar modules are made from a single crystal structure, offering higher efficiency and better performance in low-light conditions, making them suitable for space-constrained installations. In contrast, polycrystalline solar modules consist of multiple crystal structures, typically resulting in lower manufacturing costs and a more budget-friendly option, but with slightly reduced efficiency. Both types serve the growing demand for renewable energy, catering to different consumer needs and budget levels.</p></p>
