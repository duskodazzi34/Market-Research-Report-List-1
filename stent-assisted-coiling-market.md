<p><p>Stent-Assisted Coiling is a minimally invasive endovascular technique used to treat intracranial aneurysms. This procedure involves the deployment of a stent to support the artery and facilitate the placement of coils, which occlude the aneurysm and reduce the risk of rupture. The method is preferred in challenging anatomical scenarios where the aneurysm is wide-necked or located in highly branched vessels.</p><p>The Stent-Assisted Coiling Market is experiencing significant growth due to several factors, including the rising incidence of cerebrovascular diseases, advancements in medical imaging technologies, and the increasing adoption of minimally invasive surgical techniques. Furthermore, the growing awareness among healthcare professionals about the benefits of stent-assisted methods over traditional open surgeries is driving market expansion.</p><p>Current trends indicate a surge in the development of innovative stent designs and materials that improve patient outcomes and reduce complication rates. <p><strong>Market Segmentation</strong></p>
<p><strong>The Stent-Assisted Coiling Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Self-Expandable Stents</li><li>Liquid Embolic Devices</li><li>Flow-diversion Devices</li><li>Embolic Coils</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The stent-assisted coiling market encompasses various products aimed at treating intracranial aneurysms. Self-expandable stents provide scaffolding to support the vessel wall during coil placement, enhancing stability. Liquid embolic devices offer a minimally invasive option for occluding blood vessels by injecting a liquid that solidifies. Flow-diversion devices redirect blood flow away from aneurysms, promoting healing. Embolic coils, which are tiny metal coils, are used to fill the aneurysm and prevent blood flow, effectively reducing rupture risks.</p></p>
