<p><p>The Black and White Photographic Gelatin market is primarily categorized into inert gelatin and semi-inert gelatin. Inert gelatin is chemically stable and does not react with photoactive materials, providing consistent quality for traditional black and white film. On the other hand, semi-inert gelatin can undergo slight chemical changes, offering improved adhesion and emulsion characteristics. Both types serve crucial roles in photographic processes, affecting image quality, stability, and overall performance in black and white photography.</p></p>
