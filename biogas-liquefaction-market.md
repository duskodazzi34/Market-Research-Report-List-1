<p><p>The biogas liquefaction market comprises two main types: cryogenic technology and conventional upgrading technology. Cryogenic technology involves cooling biogas to extremely low temperatures, resulting in the formation of liquefied biogas (LBG) for efficient storage and transport. On the other hand, conventional upgrading technology focuses on removing impurities and CO2 from biogas to enhance its methane content, producing biomethane. Both methods aim to improve biogas's utility and marketability, catering to the growing demand for renewable energy sources.</p></p>
